# Boston_University_Urban_Wind_Turbine
Design process and testing documentation for Boston University MechE senior design project 183A: The Photonics Alley/Cummington Mall Wind Turbine

Project worked on by Mia Faillaci, Dylan List, Sofia Luce, Will Maharry, Maeve Sherlock, Miguel Silva - Boston University Department of Mechanical Engineering

Advised by Professor Gutierrez-Wing - Boston University Department of Mechanical Engineering/n

Project overview: 
Typical weather conditions in Boston, combined with Boston University’s campus architecture, create a unique wind environment where already fast moving air gets funneled into tight corridors, noticeably amplifying its speed. This increased speed provides an apparent potential for free electrical power generation via a wind turbine, though the applicability of this scenario to BU’s campus remains unknown. Through instrumenting wind speeds and directions at various wind tunnels around campus, the wind conditions at these locations will be better understood to provide initial parameters to design a turbine around. This will allow for the creation of a prototype turbine to demonstrate the ability to transform wind energy into measurable electrical power, as well as to better understand the interaction between the wind and the turbine itself. Multiple additional turbines will be designed and deployed using these prototype results to explore the design space of possible turbine sizes, blade profiles, generator options, and manufacturing methods. Based on these tests, it will be determined if BU’s campus is well-suited for an urban wind turbine, and if so, how this turbine should be designed for maximum power production and efficiency.
